COVID-19 FACE SHIELD PRODUCTION
COVID-19 Face Shield Production
Year: 2020 Medium: FDM 3D printing, laser-cut acrylic Role: Designer, Fabricator, Organizer

During the COVID-19 pandemic, I 3D-printed over 3,500 face shields and distributed them to first-responder organizations across the Bay Area. The project started with a single printer and a publicly available shield design, then scaled into a production operation—optimizing print settings, batch workflows, and material sourcing to maximize output.

The project earned a Certificate of Honor from NorCal Ambulance. More importantly, it taught me how to think about manufacturing at scale: identifying bottlenecks, iterating on process rather than just product, and coordinating logistics under real constraints.

How do I maximize throughput on a single FDM printer?
What's the minimum viable shield design for protection and comfort?
